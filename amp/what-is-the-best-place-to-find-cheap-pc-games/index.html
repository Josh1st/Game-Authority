What is the Best place to find cheap pc games?

By Joshua November 26, 2020

So if you are like me you want to play some games, but you can’t justify paying full price for a game at the major retailers. But you also have some ethics you don’t want to pirate games because you know crime doesn’t pay. If that’s you, then I have just the list of places to get cheap pc games for you as well as strategies to save you money on your gaming journey.

(some of the links provided may or may not be affiliate links, using them helps me out a bit)

 

Cdkeys

Link: https://www.cdkeys.com/?mw_aref=GameAuthority

I have been using Cdkeys for a long time now and in my opinion, I think they are the best key reseller around. They always have good prices, always feature some sweet deals, There interface is clean and easy to use but most importantly they are not grey market key resellers. Cdkeys buy their own keys and resell them, third parties cannot sell their (potentially stolen) CD keys on their site.

 

Humble Bundle

Link: https://www.humblebundle.com/?partner=joshypoo

Humble Bundle really needs no introduction. They have been around for a very long time there is no questioning their trustworthiness. With Humble Bundle you do not need to subscribe to their bundles but if you do you do get to support a charity and can get decent discounts when buying games. They too have some pretty good deals available.

 

Green Man Gaming

Link: www.greenmangaming.com

 

Green Man Gaming has been around for a long time as well, they are certainly trustworthy you can view their Trustpilot profile here. They too have great deals, a surprisingly comprehensive selection and a clean and easy to use UI.

 

Warning About Grey Market Sellers

There are certain key sellers that I will not name directly, however, a few seconds of research will reveal huge controversies surrounding stolen key reselling. These sites (not mentioning names) offer a platform for people to sell their unwanted keys. The problem is that some bad actors acquire stolen credit card details on the black market and buy a large number of cd keys and resell them on these sorts of market places. The owner of these cards get the shock of their life seeing they just bought 1000 copies of Cyberpunk 2077 and get their bank to reverse the charges and the game publisher takes the monetary hit while the “reseller” still makes their money and the marketplace gets their cut. This is devastating to the developer and the end-user in the event that their key gets deactivated. That is why I recommend you avoid market places that allow other users to sell keys unless there are proper systems in place that protect against this sort of abuse.

 

 

But wait… There are alternatives you should know about

 

Before spending any money you should investigate game subscription services that give you access to a wide variety of games for a small* fee for the month (or however long you keep the subscription). The idea here is to see how long you plan to play the particular game you want to play and if you want to play more than one game. If you want multiple games, for just one month, it may make sense to get a subscription, play your games and then cancel. Or if you want to just play one game but not pay full price you can get the subscription and cancel and have the benefit of other games if you change your mind.

For example, I want to play watch dogs legion but I don't want to pay the full price and I only have December off to play games. It makes little sense to me to buy the game at full price and only play it for one month and have it sit on my ssd. So ill rather get the Ubisoft plus subscription for one month play the heck out of watch dogs and if I have extra time I would like to play some of the assassins creed games that I've missed out on over the years.

Another Example, when EA finally releases a modern battlefield (I'm not a fan of the old war stuff), I will buy it at full price because I will probably spend a few hours a week playing it after work or on weekends for multiple months. Therefore keeping a subscription for so long may not make that much sense.

Anyway here are the most notable subscription providers are (in no particular order):

EA Play

 
Link: https://www.ea.com/ea-play/games-ea-play

EA Play comes in 2 flavours, basic and pro. Basic features almost all of EAs games but generally excludes some of their latest releases. Pro includes Basic plus their latest offerings. It would be an easy sell if EA actually had decent games * mic drop *… coughs * andromeda *, coughs again * anthem *. Jokes aside, EA seems like they get the picture now that they can fleece their customers, so the attractiveness of EA Play will increase.

Xbox Game Pass PC

Link: https://www.xbox.com/en-ZA/xbox-game-pass/pc-games

 

Arguably the best value for money out of the lot, it's cheap and gives you access to over 100 games. obviously, not all the games are that great, but a lot of them are premium AAA games. Xbox game pass also gives you access to game streaming which may be advantageous if you have weaker pc hardware or if you want to ignore your grandma at Christmas dinner by dumpstering noobs on sea of thieves or rainbow six siege on your mobile phone, Xbox game streaming makes it possible.

 

Ubisoft Plus

Link: https://store.ubi.com/ie/ubisoftplus

 

Ubisoft Plus is the most expensive of all the subscriptions listed, but the games available are pretty much all very good games. Xbox game pass pc has a few good games but a considerable amount of games I wouldn't play. But come to Ubisoft Plus they have more good games than bad games. Obviously, that is completely subjective, the price may be higher, but I think the value is there.

You should also keep your eye on the Ubisoft launcher as they give away quite a few games.

 

Epic Games Store

Link: https://www.epicgames.com/store/en-US/free-games

 

Epic Games does not have a subscription but it is worth installing the launcher and enabling notifications for free games as they often give away high-quality games. It cost you nothing so might as well.
